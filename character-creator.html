Basic Information

Questions to spark ideas:

  • What does their name mean, and did they choose it or was it given to them?
  • Do they go by a nickname, title, or alias? Why?
  • How does their species/race affect how society treats them?
  • Is their occupation a calling, a survival necessity, or a cover story?
  • Do they align with their faction's values, or are they a reluctant member?
  • For non-human ages: what milestones or events mark time for their species?
🧠

Personality

Core character questions:

  • Is this character shaped more by nature or nurture? What single event changed them forever?
  • What do they want the world to think of them vs. who they actually are?
  • Are they aware of their own flaws? Do they embrace or deny them?
  • What's their relationship with authority — do they follow, lead, or resist?
  • When pushed to the edge, do they explode, shut down, or get eerily calm?
  • What would they sacrifice — and what would they never give up?

Archetype prompts: Warrior (fights for something), Sage (seeks truth), Rebel (breaks rules), Guardian (protects others), Shadow (operates in darkness), Jester (uses humor as armor), Lover (defined by connection), Hero (rises to challenges).

For Internal Conflict: What two parts of themselves are at war? (e.g., loyalty vs. freedom, love vs. duty, control vs. desire)

Communication Style

Finding their voice:

  • Do they say more than they mean, or less? Are they direct or evasive?
  • Do they use humor to deflect, or are they almost incapable of jokes?
  • How does stress change their speech — do they get quieter or louder?
  • Do they have a verbal tic, a phrase they return to, a word they overuse?
  • How do they talk to people they respect vs. people they don't?
  • Do they use pet names, formal titles, or avoid names entirely?
  • What language do their endearments come from, and why that language?
⚔️

Skills & Abilities

Building a believable skillset:

  • How did they acquire these skills — training, survival, inheritance, accident?
  • Is there a skill gap between what they can do and what they admit to?
  • What's their fighting philosophy — brutal efficiency, elegant precision, or wild improvisation?
  • For supernatural abilities: what does using them cost them? (exhaustion, pain, emotion, years of life?)
  • What's something they're embarrassingly bad at that contradicts their image?
  • Is there a skill they want but don't have? Something they're working toward?
❤️

Preferences

Preference questions:

  • What small, mundane thing genuinely brings them joy that would surprise people?
  • Is there something they hate that used to be meaningful to them?
  • Do their dislikes reveal their values — or their trauma?
  • Are they romantically cautious or do they fall hard and fast?
  • What does intimacy mean to them beyond the physical — what do they crave emotionally?
  • Is there a contradiction between what they say they want and what they actually respond to?
🏠

Living Situation

Space and possessions reveal character:

  • Does their living space reflect who they are, or hide it?
  • Is there something they own that they would die before losing?
  • Do they feel at home where they live, or are they always ready to leave?
  • What does their vehicle (or lack of one) say about their freedom or lack thereof?
  • Do they have anything that belonged to someone they lost?
✨

Appearance

Appearance questions:

  • What's the first thing people notice about them — is that intentional?
  • Do their scars, tattoos, or marks have stories? Do they hide or display them?
  • Does their clothing tell you who they are, or who they want people to think they are?
  • How does their alt/shifted form differ emotionally from their base form?
  • What does their scent say about them — is it comforting, threatening, intoxicating?
  • How do they carry themselves — do they take up space or try to disappear?
📖

Background

Backstory questions:

  • What is the defining wound of their past — the thing they've never fully healed from?
  • Was there a moment they made a choice they still regret? One they're proud of?
  • Who raised them and how did that shape (or damage) them?
  • What did they have to become to survive? Did it cost them something?
  • Where are they right now, emotionally and circumstantially, when the story begins?
  • What do they believe the future holds — do they have hope, dread, or numbness?

Sample Dialogue

Tips for writing good example dialogue:

  • Write at least one exchange that shows how they handle tension or conflict.
  • Include one that shows warmth or vulnerability — not just their surface persona.
  • Show how they react to being challenged, questioned, or pushed.
  • Use their signature phrases, speech patterns, and vocabulary naturally.
  • Format: {{user}}: [message] followed by [Character name]: [response]
